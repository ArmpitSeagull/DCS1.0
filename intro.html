Introduction

The world has changed dramatically -

in the last 30 years. The rise of the internet means that much of our lives are now conducted online, and the process of digitizing society only seems to be accelerating. With this rapid change comes the need to know about digital citizenship and data security, roles, responsibilities and skills to navigate digital life. Data must be protected against unauthorized access, modification or deletion.

This involves ensuring that data is protected in transit, at rest, and at end-of-life (ie, sanitizing or effectively destroying storage media after use). In many cases, the data will be outside your direct control, so it is important to consider the protections you can apply, as well as the assurances you need from third parties.
