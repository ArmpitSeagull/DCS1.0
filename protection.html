How They Protect Our Data -

Here we will talk about how our data is protected from a law and legislation perspective. There are a few things in place to make sure the vast amounts of data that is collected on people and businesses is not unlawfully used for the purpose of personal gain or to hurt individuals or organisations.


We will talk about the Data Protection Act of 2018, which is the UK’s implementation of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The Data Protection Act 2018 is a set of rules and a law that helps to keep you safe when your data is being used by organisation, businesses and the government.

Everyone who collects, analyses and stores personal data must follow these strict rules.

  • Fairly, lawfully and Transparently
  • Used for Specific and Explicit Purposes
  • Used in a way the is Adequate, Relevant and limited to only what is necessary
  • Accurate and kept Up to Date
  • Kept for no Longer Than Is Necessary
  • Handled in a way that ensures appropriate security, including protection against unlawful or unauthorised processing, access, loss, destruction or damage.

Learning these 6 principles is the best start to learning the Data Protection Act.

There is strong legal protection for more sensitive personal Information such as.

  • Race
  • Ethnic background
  • Political opinions religious beliefs
  • Genetics
  • Trade union membership
  • Biometrics
  • Health
  • Sex life or orientation

Your Rights -

Under the data protection act 2018 you have the right to find out what information the government and other organisation store about you. These include the right to


  • Be informed about how your date is being used.
  • Access Personal Data
  • Have Incorrect Data updated
  • Have data Erased
  • Stop or Restrict the Processing of your Data
  • Data portability (allowing you to get and reuse your data for different services)
  • Object to How your Data is being Processed in certain circumstances

You also have the right -

to know when an organisation is using your personal data for


  • Automated Decision-Making Processes
  • Profiling

There is also some data protection rights enlisted into the human rights act 1998 (Technically 2nd of October 2000) with in this human rights act there is article 8: That all public bodies are to respect the private life of an individual and any information help about them. They must be able to justify storing or processing of any personal data. This has less of an impact than the Data Protection Act but its all there to help us stay safe.

How We Can Protect Our Data.

When it comes to protecting our data it’s important to remember we have the right to control over your data. You have the right to ask an organisation or public body to have a copy of your data that they are storing about you. When you ask for your data it should only take about a month to receive it. If it takes more than a month, they should tell you why.


You would write to the organisation or if it’s a public body you would right write to their Data Protection Officer.

However, they can withhold information for certain circumstances for example.

  • The prevention, detection or investigation of a crime
  • National security or the armed forces
  • The assessment or collection of tax
  • Judicial or ministerial appointment

There might be an administrative charge for the request of the data if you’re asking for a large amount of information or your request will take a lot time and effort to process

The amount of data that we give companies for free without thinking how much it can affect us is very large. The biggest industry for collecting personal data is social medias. Facebook, Instagram and TikTok all collect loads of personal data and this can be used to target you with specific adds or even be used to track you and your hobbies or routines.
