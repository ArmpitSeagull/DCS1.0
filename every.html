Data

in Everyday Life

Intro

Data is and always will be a part of our lives. It can be recorded in history, it can predict the future but when it comes to our everyday life, we don’t even know its there. Or at least we don’t see it as data. Things like loyalty cards for supermarkets or contacts in your phone. These things are just tools and ways to save money but in fact it is data that is collected and stored in memory to be used for a purpose to help someone one way or another.


Data has an impact on almost every part of our lives from travel to health, financial and business. It’s a vital part of our lives and we probably take it for granted as its just an everyday thing now. We all use a web browser to search for information and we are never amazed that we found a whole load of information on the one thing we searched for. That’s because data has value and there is a wealth of value stored away in databases all over the globe.

Big Data & US

That’s when we start to learn about BIG DATA. Big data is a term used to describe large quantities of data that is collected and stored in a data base about a particular subject, topic or research. It an important part of the development of businesses, it helps move science and technologies forwards and it also helps with society and then medical front.

How big data affects us a people in our everyday life isn’t apparent because we are consumers of the results an not really a part of the collection and use of big data. Other than giving data to be analysed and understood by organisation and businesses we don’t see things the same way these entities do. For example. You have a loyalty card with a supermarket and when you use this card to buy your shopping it sends data to the company on what you bought how often you buy these things and what time and date you were shopping. Now imagine 100’s of people are sending this data to this supermarket and the amount of data that is collected. That’s big data.

This company can analyse this data and look for trends and patterns then make decisions based on the results. This is why you find that deals go on and off offers periodically because they know roughly when you get paid, when there is peak shoppers buying specific items such as the weekly essentials, monthly shops. What items are popular and what items are worth phasing out.


This is just one example of a great many that can impact our lives. A few other would be medical records and how many people in an area visit the doctors and hospitals. This can help them think of budgets and facilities necessary to keep up with demand. The governments store vast amounts of data on housing and population, business trends and public services. This all helps us with having the appropriate infrastructure for living a higher quality of life, in terms of having places to live and shop as well as being able to get from one place to another.
