How data helps the world

Data Where?

Data is pretty much the undercurrent of everything we do in todays age. Every time you check the weather, track your food or a run, book a hotel or shop online, data is behind it all.


Data isn’t just here to personalise our experiences though, it is also being used in new ways in sustainability, wildlife conservation and to help the world. GPS tagging and camera traps are being used to collect real-time data on the movements of some of the worlds most critically endangered animals, enabling conservation groups to potentially save species. This data also allows data scientists to track migration, population growth/decline and identify risk that may threaten species. Researchers also use data to monitor the weather, predict forecasts and look at the effects of global warming, by looking in detail at the speed at which the ice caps are melting..

Data also helps our world through saving lives. Big data is used by the medical industry to improve research, patient outcomes and produce lifesaving technology.


In Medical Research

data is used to help researchers in labs, leading to breakthroughs in how we understand and treat disease. In hospitals, they use data in all of the hospital operations they cover. Hospitals use data much like any other business. They monitor staff, supplies, waiting room times and insurance claim data.

Patient’s outcomes and information is recorded as data and used to improve the treatment given in hospitals, how to treat individual patients, as well as answering big questions. By collecting large amounts of data and gaining a holistic view of patient’s health, medical professionals are able to give recommendations for preventive care and stop diseases.

Data is also ever present in the business world, which really does affect the way in which our world goes round.
There simply aren’t enough hours in the day to list all the companies in every corner of the world that rely on data in some way, from customer demographics informing marketing and advertising decisions, to product usage data that informs UI decisions and teams to create and build better digital experiences.
Big data helps big tech launch new features, improve existing products and help launch completely new innovations.

In all, data helps business leaders identify problems to solve and needs to fill.



Data helps us to understand the state of our world and may provide insights to save us.
